Connected

The more I observe, the more I think there is no individuality in this universe. Everything works as a connected system. We just don't see it with naked eyes.

📅 June 2026 📍 Brussels ⏱ 3 min read

Brussels, June 2026.

How are we all connected? All of us. I keep asking this. And the more I observe, the more I think there is no individuality in this universe. Everything works as a connected system. We just don't see it with naked eyes.

Start with the body. The brain works one way. The stomach works another. The stomach even has its own microbiome, and almost its own brain. They are very different. But they are not separate. They keep sending signals back to each other, in a loop, like one engine. And that is how we survive.

I think the whole of life is like this. Nothing alive stands alone.

How we feel another person's pain. How we show empathy. How we cry, laugh, love, long, and suffer. We never really do it alone.

How birds migrate from one side of the world to the other. How bees hum around the flowers and carry life from one to the next. Nobody tells them to. They are following a season, a pull they cannot see.

How the weather changes. How day becomes night. How the seasons come and go, every year, on time.

And it is not only here. The Earth stays in its place because of gravity. The universe holds together even as it keeps expanding, drifting towards infinity, towards nothingness. Maybe there is dark energy, or particles, or something we have not found yet, holding it all together. I don't know. It is yet to be discovered.

Everything that looks disconnected is, in the end, connected.

But I keep noticing the same thing, big and small. Everything that looks disconnected is, in the end, connected. And that is what keeps the very existence afloat.

These are part of my curious mind, and what I keep observing.

✦ ✦ ✦

Brussels — June 2026
