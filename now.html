Now

A page about what I'm actually thinking about, reading, and noticing — right now. Not a plan, not a promise. Just a window.

📅 Last updated: April 2026 🔄 Updated roughly once a month
This is a Now page, an idea I borrowed from Derek Sivers. The rule is simple: if we met for coffee and you asked me "what are you up to these days?", this is what I would tell you. No performance, no résumé, no strategy — just the honest answer, updated when my answer changes.
🚶

Walking

Most mornings and evenings, to and from the office. Longer walks on weekends in Parc Josaphat, Bois de la Cambre, or the Forêt de Soignes when I have a full afternoon. The walks have become less about exercise and more about looking — at plants, at light, at patterns I have ignored for years.

Recently stopped by the lilacs. That became an essay.

Writing

Publishing a new Field Note roughly every two weeks. So far: Observe and How Does the Lilac Know?. The third one is taking shape in my voice notes, somewhere between symmetry and memory.

I write slowly and in short bursts. Most essays start as a voice note on a walk, then get typed up over a quiet evening.

💼

Working

Program management in cloud transformation — currently focused on platform engineering, Kubernetes modernisation, and AI infrastructure readiness. The day job and the writing live side by side, and I am slowly learning that they feed each other rather than compete.

🌀

Wondering

Whether what plants do when they decide to flower counts as a kind of intelligence, even without neurons. Whether the same mathematics of learning shows up in statistics, in ecosystems, and in the AI models we are building. Whether patience is actually the most undervalued scientific instrument.

No clean answers. Good company.
